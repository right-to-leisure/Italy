What Is the Right to Leisure?

Four building blocks that make up a full, free life

🕐

Rest & Limits on Work

Everyone has the right to reasonable working hours, so that life isn't consumed by labour alone.

🏖️

Paid Holidays

Periodic paid leave ensures workers can recharge without fear of losing income or their job.

🎨

Free Time to Thrive

Time away from work to pursue hobbies, family, creativity, and personal growth.

⚽

Participation in Culture

Access to sport, arts, community events, and cultural life is part of leisure too.

🌍

Universal Access

This right applies to everyone — regardless of gender, age, income, or where you live.

Article 24

The heart of the right — straight from the 1948 Universal Declaration

"Everyone has the right to rest and leisure, including reasonable limitation of working hours and periodic holidays with pay."
Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Article 24 — UN, 1948

Why It Matters

Leisure builds healthier people, families, and societies

Mental & Physical Health

Adequate rest lowers stress, reduces the risk of burnout, improves sleep, and boosts mental well-being. Play is not idleness — it is recovery.

Stronger Families & Communities

Free time allows people to invest in relationships, raise children, care for relatives, and build the bonds that hold society together.

Personal Development

Leisure enables learning, creativity, and self-discovery outside formal work or education — from reading to sport to art.

A Check on Exploitation

Without this right, workers can be forced into excessive hours with no relief. Article 24 exists to protect human dignity at work.
